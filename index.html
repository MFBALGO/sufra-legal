Sufra — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 June 2026

Sufra (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a household meal-planning app. This policy explains what information the app collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. We keep this short and plain because we collect very little.

Who this policy is for

Sufra is designed for a household: one person who plans meals (the “planner”) and a cook who views the day’s menu on a kitchen tablet. This policy applies to everyone who uses the app.

What we collect

We only collect what the app needs to work. We do not use advertising, analytics, or tracking of any kind, and we never sell your data.

  • Email address — used to sign you in with a one-time code (OTP). It is your account identifier.
  • Account identifier — a unique ID assigned to your account so the app knows which household and dishes are yours.
  • Names you add — your display name and the names of household members and groupings you create.
  • Photos — any photo you attach to a dish.
  • Content you create — your dishes, ingredients, prep notes, menus, headcounts, and shopping lists.

We do not collect your location, contacts, device identifiers, advertising identifiers, browsing history, or usage analytics.

How we use your information

We use the information only to provide the app’s features — to sign you in, save your dishes and meal plans, scale recipes to your headcount, show the day’s menu on the cook’s display, and build your shopping list. We do not use your information for advertising, marketing, profiling, or analytics.

How your information is shared

We do not sell or rent your data. We share information only with the service providers that make the app work:

  • Supabase — secure cloud hosting and database where your account and content are stored (data centre in Tokyo, Japan).
  • Google (Gmail) — delivers your one-time sign-in code to your email address.
  • Google — only when you choose to import a recipe from a YouTube link, the link’s recipe content is sent to Google’s APIs to read the recipe. No account data is sent.
  • Apple — distributes the app.

These providers process data on our behalf and are not permitted to use it for their own purposes.

How we protect your information

Sign-in uses a one-time code, the cook’s display is connected with a short, time-limited pairing code, and your content is stored in a database with per-household access rules so one household cannot see another’s data. Photos are served through short-lived private links.

Data retention and deletion

We keep your information for as long as you use the app. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time from inside the app (More → Delete account). You can also request deletion by emailing us at the address below, and we will remove it.

Children’s privacy

Sufra is intended for adults managing a household. It is not directed at children and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the date at the top and publish the new version at this address.
